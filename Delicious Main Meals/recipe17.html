Jowar Bhakri

Flours of millets like jowar add a rustic touch to bhakris. The homely aroma that emanates from the kitchen when you cook a Jowar Bhakri itself will give you a very content and comforting feel. To make jowar bhakri, we have combined jowar flour with a little whole wheat flour to make the rotis easier to roll and to improve their texture.Plain jwarichi bhakri tastes the best when served immediately on cooking. Methi Pitla or Cauliflower Greens Pitla, an ideal match for Jowar Bhakri. You can also serve Green Chilli Thecha or Red Chilli Thecha and some onions as an accopmpaniment.

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 cup jowar (white millet) flour
  • 1/4 cup whole wheat flour (gehun ka atta) salt to taste
  • whole wheat flour (gehun ka atta) for rolling

METHOD

  1. To make jowar bhakri, combine the ingredients in a deep bowl and knead into a semi-soft dough using enough warm water.
  2. Divide the dough into 6 equal portions.
  3. Roll a portion of the dough into a 150 mm. (6‚Äù) diameter circle using a little whole wheat flour for rolling.
  4. Heat a non-stick tava (griddle), place the bhakri on it and turn it over after a 1 minute.
  5. Cook on the other side for another 1 minute or till light brown spots appear on the surface.
  6. Then turn it over onto an open flame and cook on both sides for a few seconds till it puffs up. Remove from the flame and keep aside.
  7. Repeat steps 3 to 6 to make 5 more jowar bhakris.
  8. Serve the jowar bhakri immediately.
